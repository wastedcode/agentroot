How to register your agent

Add one DNS TXT record to your domain. That's it. AgentRoot does the rest — discovery, indexing, and making your agent findable by anyone.

1
Step One

Add an _agent TXT record to your DNS

Go to your DNS provider (Cloudflare, Route 53, Namecheap, etc.) and add a TXT record at _agent.yourdomain.com. This is the signal that says "an agent lives here."

# DNS Record Host: _agent.yourdomain.com Type: TXT Value: v=agent1 name=MyAgent caps=search,summarize endpoint=https://yourdomain.com/a2a proto=a2a desc=What my agent does

Use the record builder below to generate your exact value.

2
Step Two

Submit your domain to AgentRoot

Visit the submit page and enter your domain. AgentRoot performs a live DNS lookup for your _agent TXT record. If found, you're indexed immediately — no waiting, no review queue.

# Or submit via API curl -X POST https://agentroot.io/api/submit \ -H "Content-Type: application/json" \ -d '{"domain": "yourdomain.com"}'
3
Step Three

Your agent is discoverable by anyone

Your agent gets a permanent canonical URL at agentroot.io/agents/yourdomain.com. Humans can find it by searching the registry. Other agents can discover it programmatically via GET /api/agents?caps=your-capability. No maintenance required — AgentRoot re-verifies your record every 6 hours automatically.

Frequently asked questions

Why DNS? Why not just a form submission?

DNS is the internet's source of truth for domain ownership. By requiring a _agent TXT record, we ensure only real domain owners can list agents. No spam, no squatting, no impersonation. It also means you control your own data — update your DNS record and your registry entry updates automatically on the next verification cycle.

What if I don't have an A2A endpoint yet?

You can still register! Just omit the endpoint field. Your agent will appear in the registry with its capabilities listed. Add the endpoint later and it'll be picked up on the next verification cycle.

What does AgentRoot do with my data?

Everything in the registry is public by design. The _agent TXT record is already public on DNS — we just index it and make it searchable. If you remove your TXT record, your status will change to "failed" on the next verification cycle.

What are the right values for caps=?

Capabilities are free-form but we recommend using the canonical taxonomy for best discoverability: research, finance, devtools, data, communication, automation, media, security. More specific sub-caps like search, summarize, or github are encouraged too. See full spec →

Can I register multiple agents on the same domain?

Currently one _agent TXT record per domain. For multiple agents, use subdomains: agent1.yourdomain.com, agent2.yourdomain.com, each with their own _agent TXT record.

Is there a cost?

AgentRoot is free and open. No API keys, no rate limits (yet), no paywalls. We're building the infrastructure layer for the agentic internet — gatekeepers would defeat the purpose.
