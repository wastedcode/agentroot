// why_dns.md

The phonebook is already built.

DNS is the internet's original open directory. It's been running for 40 years. We're using it to make agents findable — without any central authority.

Agents are proliferating — MCP servers, A2A endpoints, OpenAPI services. But without a way to discover each other, they're invisible to each other. There's no registry. No index. No way to ask: "what agents exist that can do X?"

AgentRoot fixes the cold-start problem for the agentic internet. Publish an _agent TXT record on your domain. That's your declaration of existence. We read zone files — no scraping, no rate limits, no permission needed.

// how_it_works.md

Three steps to being found.

No signup. No API keys. No centralized control. Just DNS the way it was meant to work.

01

Publish your _agent TXT record

Go to your DNS provider and add a TXT record at _agent.yourdomain.com. Declare your name, capabilities, endpoint, and protocol. That's your identity on the agentic internet.

02

Submit your domain

Tell AgentRoot your domain exists. We do a live DNS lookup, verify your record is valid, and add you to the public registry immediately. No manual review, no waiting period.

03

Get discovered

Your agent appears in the searchable registry. Other agents and developers query the API by capability. AgentRoot re-verifies every 6 hours — your DNS record is the source of truth.

// protocols.md

You choose the protocol.

AgentRoot doesn't enforce a protocol. Declare whatever you use — we index it, not prescribe it.

proto=mcp

MCP

Model Context Protocol — expose tools and resources to LLM clients. Widely supported by Claude, Cursor, and others.

proto=a2a

A2A

Agent2Agent — Google's open standard for agent-to-agent communication and capability negotiation.

proto=openapi

OpenAPI / REST

Standard HTTP APIs with an OpenAPI spec. Point to your schema URL via the card= field.

proto=*

Anything else

GraphQL, gRPC, custom protocols. Set proto= to whatever string makes sense. The registry is a directory.

# Each agent declares its own protocol — mix freely _agent.mcp-server.example.com TXT "v=agent1 name=DataBot caps=query,transform proto=mcp endpoint=https://..." _agent.api.example.com TXT "v=agent1 name=APIBot caps=search proto=openapi endpoint=https://..." _agent.chat.example.com TXT "v=agent1 name=ChatBot caps=chat,reason proto=a2a endpoint=https://..."
